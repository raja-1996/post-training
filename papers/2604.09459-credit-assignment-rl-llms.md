## Background: why credit assignment is the central problem

### The two waves of LLM RL

- **Reasoning RL (2023–2025):** single chain-of-thought trajectory, 500–30K+ tokens (DeepSeek-R1 averages ~23K tokens on AIME 2025). Reward purely terminal/verifiable ("is the final answer correct?"). DeepSeek-R1 and OpenAI o1/o3 are the headline recipes.
- **Agentic RL (2024–present):** 10–100+ turns, total token counts of 100K–1M+. One reported SWE-bench setup: agents average ~64 turns consuming ~131K tokens (Wang et al. 2025d, RAGEN).
- A **third wave (multi-agent)** is rising (2026+).

### Why CA is the bottleneck

Sparse terminal reward means a single bit (or scalar) must be distributed across thousands of tokens or hundreds of turns. The variance of the REINFORCE estimator with a constant baseline scales as $O(T \cdot \mathrm{Var}[R])$. Moving from $T=10$ to $T=100$ inflates gradient variance 10×; with $T \sim 100$ and binary reward, signal-to-noise per action is ~100× worse than single-turn reasoning. Empirically Wang et al. 2025d show this as the **"echo trap"**: under episode-level credit, agents converge to repetitive safe behaviors.

### Three historical phases (Figure 1)

| Phase | Period | Reward | Trajectory | CA |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| RLHF (InstructGPT) | 2022–23 | dense RM | single turn, ~500 tok | implicit (PPO critic) |
| Reasoning RL (R1, o1) | 2023–25 | outcome 0/1 | single gen, 0.5K–30K tok | token/step (PRM) |
| Agentic RL | 2024–pres. | sparse terminal | multi-turn, 100K–1M tok | turn / hindsight |
| Future Multi-Agent | 2026+ | team reward | 100+ turns | cross-agent |

### MDP formalism

**Reasoning RL as token-level MDP.** Generation $y=(y_1,\ldots,y_L)$ given prompt $x$.
- State $s_t = (x, y_1, \ldots, y_{t-1})$
- Action $a_t = y_t$
- Transition deterministic (autoregression)
- Reward $R$ only at terminal state.

CA question: which tokens (or token groups) caused correctness?

**Agentic RL as turn-level POMDP.** $T$ turns of agent–environment interaction.
- State $s_t$ includes conversation history + partially observed env state + retrieved context.
- Action $a_t$ is the agent's complete turn (many tokens).
- Transition is stochastic (tool/web/API).
- Reward $R$ is sparse and terminal.

CA is **doubly hierarchical**: (1) which turn was critical? (2) within that turn, which tokens mattered?

### Notation (Table 1)

| Symbol | Meaning |
|---|---|
| $x$ | prompt |
| $y=(y_1,\ldots,y_L)$ | generated token sequence |
| $\tau$ | full trajectory |
| $s_t, a_t, o_t$ | state, action, observation |
| $R(\tau)$ | terminal/episodic reward |
| $r_t$ | intermediate reward |
| $V(s), Q(s,a)$ | value functions |
| $\hat{A}_t = Q(s_t,a_t) - V(s_t)$ | advantage |
| $\pi_\theta, \pi_\mathrm{ref}$ | policy, reference |
| $T$ | # turns; $L$ # tokens |
| $G$ | GRPO group size |
| $K$ | # rollouts/agents |
| $\gamma, \lambda$ | discount, GAE param |
| $c_t$ | credit at step/turn $t$ |
| $H(\cdot)$ | Shannon entropy |

### The multi-granularity action hierarchy

$$\tau = \underbrace{[\mathrm{Turn}_1,\ldots,\mathrm{Turn}_T]}_{\text{Turn level}} = \underbrace{[\mathrm{Seg}_{1,1},\ldots]}_{\text{Segment level}} = \underbrace{[a_{1,1,1},\ldots]}_{\text{Token level}}$$

### Why GRPO's episode-level credit is insufficient

GRPO computes a group-mean advantage and assigns it identically to **every token** in $\tau_i$:

$$\hat{A}^{\mathrm{GRPO}}_i = R(\tau_i) - \frac{1}{G}\sum_{j=1}^{G} R(\tau_j) \tag{2}$$

This works "reasonably" for $L\sim 10^3$–$10^4$ (reasoning), because the number of critical decisions is small relative to total tokens. It collapses for $L\sim 10^5$–$10^6$ (agentic): "choose the right API" and "format the output" receive the same gradient signal.

## Taxonomy / organizing axes

**The paper's central organizing axis is granularity × methodology — a 2-D taxonomy** (Figure 2). Granularity is the *primary* axis (the section structure mirrors it: token → segment → step → turn → multi-agent); methodology is the secondary, cross-cutting axis.

### Granularity axis (vertical in Figure 2)
- **Token-level** — individual tokens within a generation.
- **Segment-level** — semantically meaningful spans (e.g., one reasoning step boundary).
- **Step / Turn-level** — a complete LLM response or tool-call cycle.
- **Multi-agent level** — credit decomposed across collaborating agents.

### Methodology axis (horizontal)
- **Monte Carlo (MC)** — rollouts from intermediate states.
- **Temporal Difference (TD)** — learned value functions + bootstrapping.
- **Model-based / LLM-as-Critic** — LLMs evaluate intermediate states (distinctively LLM-era, no classical analogue).
- **Game-theoretic** — Shapley values, counterfactual baselines.
- **Information-theoretic** — info gain, entropy.

The dashed arrow in Figure 2 indicates the evolutionary trend from fine-grained reasoning (upper-left) toward coarser but environment-aware agentic methods (lower-right). The densest cluster is at the **Step/Turn level** — the natural action granularity for agents.

### Hierarchical taxonomy (Figure 3) summary

- **Reasoning RL → Token-Level**: VinePPO (MC), RED (Redistribution), T-REG (Self-gen.), From r to Q* (Implicit).
- **Reasoning RL → Segment-Level**: SPO (MC), SCAR (Shapley), TEMPO (Tree-TD).
- **Reasoning RL → Step-Level**: PURE (PRM), SPRO (Masked Adv.), CAPO (LLM-Critic), ACPO (Attribution), HICRA (Hierarchy), PRL (Entropy), InT (Intervention), FinePO (Fine PRM).
- **Agentic RL → Turn-Level PRM**: AgentPRM (TD+GAE), SWEET-RL (Privileged Critic), Turn-PPO (Turn MDP), SORL (Bias-corr.), TARL (LLM-Judge), ITPO (Implicit).
- **Agentic RL → Hindsight / Counterfactual**: HCAPO, C3 (LOO), CCPO (Causal), CriticSearch (Retrospective).
- **Agentic RL → Critic-Free Step-Level**: GiGPO (Group-in-Group), POAD (Action Decomp.), CARL (Entropy), iStar (Implicit DPO), IGPO (Info-theoretic).
- **Agentic RL → Hierarchical**: ArCHer (TD Hierarchy), PilotRL (Progressive), StepAgent (IRL).
- **Adjacent Enablers**: SPA-RL, Lightning, RAGEN, SCRIBE, PRS, AdaptSeg.
- **Multi-Agent → Agent-Level**: M-GRPO (Hierarchical), SHARP (Shapley), MAPPA (Per-action PRM), Dr. MAS (Agent-wise Adv.), LLM-MCA (LLM-Critic), QLLM (LLM-gen.).

### Sidebar: PRMs are credit assignment

A key conceptual claim: **Process Reward Models (PRMs) are not a separate "reward modelling" technique — they ARE a credit-assignment mechanism.** A PRM scoring each reasoning step $i$ with $r_i$ is performing step-level decomposition of the terminal reward $R(\tau)$. The PRM literature (Math-Shepherd, OmegaPRM, PURE) and the CA literature (VinePPO, SPRO, SCAR) are two perspectives on the same problem. The survey adopts the CA perspective throughout.

### Classical CA → LLM CA mapping
- TD/GAE → learned critics (ArCHer, AgentPRM).
- Return decomposition (RUDDER, Arjona-Medina 2019) → reward redistribution (RED, SPA-RL, IGPO).
- Hindsight (Harutyunyan 2019 HCA) → retrospective analysis (HCAPO).
- Counterfactual / difference rewards → leave-one-out and Shapley (C3, SCAR, CCPO).
- **LLM-as-Critic** has *no* direct classical analogue and is a distinctive new methodological axis.

### RL algorithms used as base
- **PPO**: token-level GAE with a learned critic; value-function quality directly limits training efficiency.
- **REINFORCE / REINFORCE++**: simplest policy gradient with mean baseline; crudest CA. REINFORCE++ adds token-level KL but no new decomposition.
- **GRPO** (Shao 2024, DeepSeek-R1): group-mean baseline, critic-free, episode-level credit (the baseline that most methods in this survey improve over).
- **DPO** (Rafailov 2023): bypasses explicit RM via preference pairs.
- **From r to Q\*** (Rafailov 2024): DPO implicitly learns a token-level Q-function — $Q^*(s_t, a_t) = \beta \log \frac{\pi_\theta(a_t|s_t)}{\pi_\mathrm{ref}(a_t|s_t)} + \beta \log Z(s_t)$.
- **RLOO, online DPO, IPO, KTO, ReST, Expert Iteration, STaR** are noted as not introducing new CA mechanisms.

## Methods catalogued

### Token-level credit assignment (reasoning RL)

**VinePPO (Kazemnejad et al., ICML 2025).** Replaces PPO's learned critic with **unbiased Monte Carlo value estimates** at every token. Exploits the cheap-rollout property of autoregressive LLMs: at token $t$ fork $K$ continuations ("vines"), evaluate each against the outcome reward, set $V(s_t) \approx \frac{1}{K}\sum_{k=1}^K R(\tau_t^{(k)})$, advantage $\hat{A}_t = R(\tau) - V(s_t)$. On GSM8K and MATH it beats PPO with learned critic, evidencing that *CA quality* is the bottleneck, not policy optimization. Cost: $O(K \cdot L)$ extra forward passes per trajectory.

**RED (Li et al. 2024a, Reward Redistribution).** Probes a pre-trained RM's hidden states with a linear regressor to predict each token's marginal reward contribution. Zero additional RL training. Works because RMs already encode token-level CA information that uniform credit ignores.

**T-REG (Zhou et al. 2024b, Token-Level Reward Regularization).** Self-supervised, no external model: the model generates both correct and incorrect solutions for the same problem; tokens whose log-probabilities differ most across the two are deemed most discriminative and receive higher credit.

**From r to Q\* (Rafailov et al. 2024).** Theoretical foundation: DPO implicitly learns token-level Q-values. Implies credit assignment may be a "free" by-product of alignment training. Drives later methods iStar and ITPO.

### Segment / sentence / span-level (reasoning RL)

**SPO (Guo et al. 2025, Segment Policy Optimization).** Partitions reasoning at semantically meaningful **cutpoints** (e.g., between setting up an equation and solving it). For each segment, compute MC advantage by comparing trajectories sharing the same prefix. Practical sweet spot between expensive token-MC and crude episode credit. Aligned with the natural step structure of math reasoning.

**TEMPO (Tran et al. 2025, Tree-Structured CA).** Generalizes the linear chain into a tree. At decision points, branches into a tree; **branch-gated TD corrections** propagate MC estimates from leaves upward through TD-style bootstrapping at internal nodes. Critic-free; multi-resolution credit signal.

**SCAR (Cao et al. 2025, Shapley CA Rewards).** Treats reasoning chain as a coalitional game where each segment is a player; the outcome reward is the game value. Credit = Shapley value (avg. marginal contribution across all orderings). Satisfies efficiency, symmetry, null-player properties. Uses sampling approximations (exact Shapley needs $2^n$ coalitions). Useful as a "gold standard" reference.

### Step-level (reasoning steps, math derivations)

#### PRM-based

**Background: Math-Shepherd (Wang et al. 2024) and OmegaPRM (Luo et al. 2024).** Pioneered automatic step-level labelling by MC continuation labeling and divide-and-conquer tree exploration respectively. Foundational, not counted in the 47.

**PURE (Cheng et al., ICML 2025).** Argues standard "sum-form" PRM credit $V(s_t) = \mathbb{E}[\sum_{t'=t}^T r_{t'}]$ is vulnerable to reward hacking (safe-but-useless intermediate steps inflate the sum). Proposes **min-form**: $V(s_t) = \mathbb{E}[\min_{t'\geq t} r_{t'}]$. Better calibration, less overoptimization.

**SPRO (Fei et al. 2025, Self-Guided Process Reward).** Self-supervised step-level credit via **Masked Step Advantage**: $c_i = P(\text{correct} \mid \text{full solution}) - P(\text{correct} \mid \text{solution} \setminus \text{step}_i)$. Leave-one-out at the step level. Reports **3.4× training efficiency over GRPO**.

**FinePO (Huang et al. 2026, part of SketchVL).** Pushes PRM to sub-step granularity for visual reasoning (chart/diagram understanding). Demonstrates fine-grained credit is feasible in structured domains.

**PRL (Yao et al. 2026, Process Reward Learning).** Derives step-level process rewards from entropy-regularized RL: the optimal process reward at each step equals the advantage under the entropy-regularized optimal policy. Provides theoretical (not just heuristic) PRM grounding.

**InT (Yang et al. 2026, Self-Proposed Interventions).** The model proposes counterfactual edits to specific steps and checks whether the outcome changes; high credit for steps whose intervention changes the result. Model-intrinsic, no external RM.

#### Attribution / curriculum

**ACPO (Yin et al. 2025).** Combines attribution-based step credit (e.g., gradient saliency) with curriculum learning: prioritize problems with clear bifurcation (concentrated credit) early; introduce diffuse-credit problems later.

#### LLM-as-Critic

**CAPO (Xie et al. 2025, Credit Assignment Policy Optimization).** Uses the LLM itself as a **GenPRM**: same LLM (or a prompted version) generates natural-language critiques per step, converted to scalar rewards. Self-contained (no separate RM/critic/rollouts) but vulnerable to self-evaluation bias; mitigated via calibration.

#### Hierarchy-aware

**HICRA (Wang et al. 2025c, Hierarchy-Aware CA).** Identifies a two-phase learning dynamic: procedural skills first, then strategic planning. Focuses credit on high-impact **planning tokens** rather than uniformly. Reasoning-RL method whose planning-vs-procedural distinction is highly transferable to agentic settings.

### Turn-level (agentic, multi-turn dialogue, tool calls)

#### Turn-level PRMs

**AgentPRM (Xi et al. 2025).** Adapts PRM to agentic by **replacing MC labeling with TD+GAE** (MC is prohibitively expensive when each rollout means re-spinning sandboxed environments). Update: $V(s_t) \leftarrow V(s_t) + \alpha[r_t + \gamma V(s_{t+1}) - V(s_t)]$. **8× sample efficiency** vs MC-based PRM training; +19.0% over ORM baseline on WebShop @8×8, +13.4% on TextCraft.

**SWEET-RL (Zhou et al. 2025, Meta/FAIR).** Introduces the **privileged (asymmetric) critic**: at training time the critic conditions on info the agent does not have at inference (ground-truth answer, full future trajectory, env state variables). Outputs turn-level reward signals; actor is DPO-optimized on standard observations. Sidesteps non-verifiability. +6.0 vs MT-DPO on ColBench Backend.

**Turn-Level Reward Design (Wei et al., NeurIPS 2025).** Hybrid: automated verification for verifiable turns (code exec, DB query); LLM-as-judge for subjective turns (planning, communication). Formalizes multi-turn agent training as MDP with heterogeneous reward sources.

**Turn-PPO (Li et al., EACL 2026).** Reformulates multi-turn RL as a **turn-level MDP**; each turn (response + env feedback) is a macro-action; turn-level value function + turn-level importance ratios replace token-level IS. Eval on WebShop and Sokoban.

**SORL (Li et al. 2025a, Stabilizing Off-Policy RL for Long-Horizon).** Turn-level importance sampling + clipping-triggered normalization. Instantiated as **SO-PPO and SO-GRPO**. Tackles granularity mismatch and high-variance off-policy updates.

**TARL (Tan et al. 2025, Turn-level Adjudicated RL).** LLM-as-judge gives turn-level evaluations; mixed-task training curriculum (mathematical problems mixed in). +6%+ pass rate on $\tau$-bench.

**ITPO (Wang et al. 2026, Implicit Turn-Level Process Rewards).** Extracts implicit turn-level rewards from log-probability changes across turns via the From-r-to-Q* lens. No separate RM. Applied to tutoring/recommendation.

#### Hindsight and counterfactual (the new agentic family)

**HCAPO (Tan et al., Mar 2026).** Post-trajectory **hindsight analysis** with LLM critic: for each turn, generate counterfactual continuations and compare expected outcomes. No environment re-execution needed — counterfactuals are entirely in the LLM's "imagination". Distinguishes lucky-but-suboptimal actions from genuinely causal ones.

**C3 (Chen et al., Mar 2026, Contextual Counterfactual Credit).** Leave-one-out (LOO): $c_t = R(\tau) - R(\tau_{\setminus t})$. LLM approximates the counterfactual rollout. Originally multi-agent, naturally extends to single-agent treating turns as "players".

**CCPO (Li et al., Mar 2026, Counterfactual Credit Policy Opt.).** Structural Causal Model view; each turn's action is a treatment, outcome is the effect; turn credit = average treatment effect, estimated via do-calculus or approximations. Provides theoretical guarantees under no-unobserved-confounder assumption (reasonable when full history is logged).

> The simultaneous appearance of HCAPO, C3, CCPO within one week of March 2026 is highlighted as a striking community convergence: retrospective counterfactual analysis as the natural paradigm for agentic CA.

**CriticSearch (Zhang et al. 2025c).** Hindsight specifically for search agents: a frozen, asymmetric LLM-critic uses privileged info (full trajectory + gold answer) to score each search turn. Improves convergence on multi-hop QA.

#### Critic-free step-level (agentic)

**GiGPO (Feng et al., NeurIPS 2025, Group-in-Group Policy Opt.).** Extends GRPO's group-comparison from episode to **step level**: outer-level groups trajectories (standard GRPO); inner-level groups steps within a trajectory by **anchor states** (steps sharing similar prefixes). Step's advantage = relative to its group mean. Critic-free. **+12.6 over GRPO on ALFWorld, +9.1 on WebShop (7B), +10.6 on WebShop (1.5B)**.

**POAD (Wen et al., May 2024, Policy Opt. with Action Decomposition).** Addresses the action-vs-token granularity mismatch in agentic. Derives **Bellman with Action Decomposition (BAD)**: integrates credit at two levels — intra-action (tokens within a turn) and inter-action (across turns). One of the earliest formulations of action-to-token decomposition for LLM agents.

#### Hierarchical (agentic)

**ArCHer (Zhou et al., ICML 2024, Actor-Critic with Hierarchical Evaluation).** Pioneer of hierarchical multi-turn RL for LLM agents. Explicit two-level architecture: high-level off-policy TD critic learns $Q^H(s_t, a_t)$ over turns; low-level on-policy actor optimizes token policy within each turn, using high-level Q as turn reward. Off-policy critic enables replay-buffer learning.

**PilotRL (Lu et al. 2025, Global Planning-Guided Progressive RL).** Three-stage progressive: plan-level RL → step-level RL → token-level RL. Credit cascades coarse-to-fine. Designed for agents that explicitly plan first.

**CARL (Shen et al., NeurIPS 2025, Critical Action RL).** Identifies **bifurcation points** by action entropy $H(\pi(\cdot|s_t))$ and focuses gradient updates only on highest-entropy actions. Reports **72% fewer gradient updates with no performance degradation**. +4.9 F1 on HotpotQA, +5.3 on 2WikiMQA vs GRPO. Embodies the "sparse credit" thesis: most actions have negligible credit and optimizing them wastes compute.

#### Information-theoretic

**IGPO (Wang et al. 2025a, Information Gain Policy Opt.).** Turn credit = info gain about task success:
$$c_t = \log P(\text{success} \mid h_{1:t}) - \log P(\text{success} \mid h_{1:t-1}) \tag{4}$$
Requires a reliable success-probability estimator.

#### Implicit / DPO-based (agentic)

**iStar (Liu et al. 2025, Implicit Step Rewards).** Trajectory-level DPO on success/failure pairs; extracts implicit per-turn rewards from log-prob ratios via From-r-to-Q*. Adds multi-level advantage fusion (turn + token). No explicit RM/critic/re-execution.

**StepAgent (Deng et al. 2024).** Implicit RL + inverse RL on expert demonstrations to infer step-level rewards; novice-to-expert curriculum.

### Infrastructure / practical methods (adjacent enablers)

**Agent Lightning (Luo et al. 2025, Microsoft Research).** **LightningRL** algorithm decomposes trajectories into transitions with a dedicated CA module; decouples agent execution from training; integrates with LangChain/AutoGen with no inference-code changes. Evaluated on text-to-SQL, RAG, math tool-use.

**RAGEN / StarPO (Wang et al. 2025d, "Star Policy Optimization").** Provides the "echo trap" diagnosis and addresses it via **uncertainty-based filtering**: downweight high-uncertainty credit estimates during updates.

**SPA-RL (Wang et al. 2025b, Stepwise Progress Attribution).** Lightweight MLP "progress estimator" $p_t \in [0,1]$ trained end-to-end alongside the policy; step credit $c_t = p_t - p_{t-1}$; supervised by $p_T = R(\tau)$. RUDDER-style. Extremely cheap.

**SCRIBE (Jiang & Ferraro 2026).** Library of "skill prototypes" (templates for common sub-tasks: search-and-extract, write-and-test-code, format-and-submit); each action is matched to the nearest prototype and scored. Mid-level structured supervision.

**LaRe (Qu et al., AAAI 2025, Latent Reward).** LLM generates **natural-language credit explanations** per step → scalar reward. Interpretable; useful for debugging.

**PRS + VSPO (Su et al. 2025).** Progressive Reward Shaping curriculum: early rewards on format correctness, later on task accuracy. VSPO prioritizes training on trajectories where credit is most informative.

**AdaptSeg / Adaptive Segment-Level Reward (Li et al. 2024b).** Semantic segmentation into balanced segments regardless of length, preventing long-trajectory credit dilution.

### Multi-agent / role-distributed

**M-GRPO (Hong et al. 2025).** Main + K sub-agents. Two-level credit: **inter-agent** (meta-advantage by comparing outcomes across team compositions) + **intra-agent** (standard GRPO within each). Supports decoupled, independent updates.

**LLM-MCA (Nagpal et al. 2025).** Replaces QMIX/VDN/COMA mixing networks with an LLM-based centralized critic that reads the full multi-agent conversation and produces semantic per-agent credit.

**QLLM (Li et al. 2025c).** Meta-level: prompt an LLM to **generate Python code** computing per-agent credit. Training-free, zero marginal cost; quality depends on LLM code generation.

**SHARP (Li et al., Feb 2026, Shapley Credit-based Optimization).** Three-component rewards: (1) global broadcast-accuracy, (2) Shapley-based marginal credit via coalition analysis across agents, (3) tool-process reward for execution efficiency. Agent-specific advantage normalized across trajectory groups. **+23.7% over single-agent, +14.1% over multi-agent baselines.**

**MAPPA (Li et al., Jan 2026, Multiagent Per-Action Process Awards).** Per-action AI-judge process rewards (don't wait for terminal outcome). **+5.0–17.5pp on AIME, +7.8–17.2pp on AMC, +16.7pp on data analysis tasks** — among the largest reported gains.

**Dr. MAS (Feng et al., Feb 2026, Stable RL for Multi-Agent LLM Systems).** Identifies that global normalization in multi-agent GRPO mismatches diverse agents' reward distributions, causing gradient spikes. Fix: **agent-wise advantage normalization** (each agent's stats only). +5.6% avg@16 on math; converges where multi-agent GRPO diverges.

**C3 (revisited).** Leave-one-out also works across agents: $c_k = R(\tau) - R(\tau_{\setminus k})$.

## Formal definitions and equations

### GRPO advantage (eq. 2)

$$\hat{A}^{\mathrm{GRPO}}_i = R(\tau_i) - \frac{1}{G}\sum_{j=1}^G R(\tau_j)$$

Same advantage for every token in $\tau_i$. Critic-free; episode-level.

### GAE (eq. 3)

$$\hat{A}_t^{\mathrm{GAE}(\gamma,\lambda)} = \sum_{l=0}^{\infty} (\gamma\lambda)^l \delta_{t+l}, \quad \delta_t = r_t + \gamma V(s_{t+1}) - V(s_t)$$

GAE interpolates TD(0) (high-bias) vs MC (high-variance). AgentPRM applies it at turn level.

### RUDDER-style return decomposition

$$c_t = \hat{R}(s_{0:t}) - \hat{R}(s_{0:t-1})$$

Drives RED, SPA-RL, IGPO.

### DPO implicit Q-function (From r to Q*)

$$Q^*(s_t, a_t) = \beta \log \frac{\pi_\theta(a_t \mid s_t)}{\pi_{\mathrm{ref}}(a_t \mid s_t)} + \beta \log Z(s_t)$$

Implies preference-trained LLMs already encode token-level credit. Underpins iStar, ITPO.

### VinePPO MC value

$$V(s_t) \approx \frac{1}{K} \sum_{k=1}^{K} R(\tau_t^{(k)}), \qquad \hat{A}_t = R(\tau) - V(s_t)$$

### PURE min-form credit

$$V(s_t) = \mathbb{E}\left[\min_{t' \geq t} r_{t'}\right] \quad \text{(min-form)} \quad \text{vs.} \quad V(s_t) = \mathbb{E}\left[\sum_{t'=t}^T r_{t'}\right] \quad \text{(sum-form)}$$

Min-form prevents reward hacking via "safe" intermediate steps.

### SPRO Masked Step Advantage

$$c_i = P(\text{correct} \mid \text{full solution}) - P(\text{correct} \mid \text{solution} \setminus \text{step}_i)$$

### IGPO Information Gain

$$c_t = \log P(\text{success} \mid h_{1:t}) - \log P(\text{success} \mid h_{1:t-1})$$

### C3 / Difference-reward / LOO credit

$$c_t = R(\tau) - R(\tau_{\setminus t}) \quad \text{(turns)} \qquad c_k = R(\tau) - R(\tau_{\setminus k}) \quad \text{(agents)}$$

### Variance scaling motivation

Variance of REINFORCE estimator with constant baseline $b$: $\mathrm{Var}[\nabla] \propto (R(\tau)-b)^2$. Total gradient variance across $T$ actions: $O(T \cdot \mathrm{Var}[R])$. So $T:10\to100$ inflates variance 10×.

## Reward signal sources discussed

- **Outcome / verifiable rewards.** Binary correctness on math, code, web nav with clear objective. The default in reasoning RL (DeepSeek-R1, o1).
- **Process Reward Models (PRMs).** Math-Shepherd, OmegaPRM, PURE — step-level scalars. Reinterpreted in this survey as **CA mechanisms**, not RM techniques.
- **LLM-as-Judge / GenPRM.** CAPO, TARL, LLM-MCA, LaRe, HCAPO — semantic natural-language evaluation by an LLM.
- **Privileged asymmetric critics.** SWEET-RL, CriticSearch — train-time-only info (ground truth, full future trajectory).
- **Implicit rewards from log-prob ratios.** From r to Q*, iStar, ITPO — no explicit RM training.
- **Hybrid rewards.** Wei et al. — verifier for verifiable turns, LLM-judge for subjective.
- **MLP progress estimators.** SPA-RL — lightweight learned signal.
- **Counterfactual / Shapley game-theoretic rewards.** C3, CCPO, SCAR, SHARP.
- **Information-theoretic credit.** IGPO, indirectly CARL (entropy).
- **Curriculum / progressive shaping.** PRS, ACPO, StepAgent — credit signal evolves during training.

## Why agentic RL fundamentally reshapes credit assignment

Six qualitative challenges (Section 4):

1. **Stochastic environment transitions.** APIs, web pages, code execution, users — all introduce randomness. Breaks VinePPO-style cheap MC; inflates TD error variance. Pushes the field toward **hindsight** rather than forward.
2. **Partial observability.** Database query results, ls/cat output, hidden agent states, JS state. CA must disentangle **decision errors vs. information gaps vs. exploratory actions** — largely unaddressed.
3. **Vastly longer horizons.** Reasoning $T\sim 10$ → agentic $T\sim 100$ → variance up 10×. Causes the **echo trap** (RAGEN). Causal chain action→outcome becomes indirect.
4. **Heterogeneous action types.** Planning, tool selection, tool parameterization, communication, error recovery, bookkeeping. Wildly different credit profiles; episode-level credit conflates them. Motivates CARL (entropy-focused) and HICRA (planning vs procedural).
5. **Non-verifiable intermediate states.** Step-level PRMs that work for math fail for tool calls / navigation / code generation. Drives hindsight (HCAPO), implicit (iStar), privileged (SWEET-RL).
6. **The bifurcation point problem.** Rare but decisive "forks in the road"; non-obvious in advance. Two strategies: identify them (CARL via entropy, HICRA via role) or evaluate retrospectively (HCAPO, C3).

## Comparisons / when each granularity helps

### Four trade-offs (Section 7.4)

1. **Granularity vs. computational cost [strong empirical].** Finer = more precise but costlier. VinePPO $O(KL)$ extra passes; SCAR exponential coalitions. Turn-level (CARL, SWEET-RL) is the practical agentic sweet spot.
2. **Forward estimation vs. hindsight [authors' synthesis].** Forward (PRM, VinePPO, AgentPRM) requires re-execution or learned approximations. Hindsight (HCAPO, C3, CCPO) has strict info advantage but adds latency and possible hindsight bias.
3. **Auxiliary model requirements [strong empirical].** No aux (CARL, iStar, GiGPO) → MLP (SPA-RL) → critic/PRM (ArCHer, AgentPRM, PURE) → LLM-scale (CAPO, HCAPO, LLM-MCA).
4. **Reasoning-specific vs. agent-general [limited but suggestive].** Reasoning methods assume determinism + verifiable steps; agentic methods (HCAPO, SWEET-RL, CARL, GiGPO) drop those assumptions.

### Descriptive pattern (n=13 data points)

Among GRPO-baseline methods: **agentic subset shows higher mean Δ (+8.5, n=5, 2 methods) than reasoning subset (+6.0, n=8, 4 methods)**. Pattern is robust to single-outlier removal. Consistent with — though not proof of — the prediction that episode-level credit degrades more severely as trajectory length grows. Confounded by base-model heterogeneity, headroom differences, and GiGPO dominating agentic data (3/5 points).

### Decision tree (Figure 4) — practical selection

- **Reasoning, short CoT (≤5K):** GRPO, PURE, SPO, SPRO.
- **Reasoning, long CoT (>5K), limited compute:** HICRA, CAPO, SPRO.
- **Reasoning, long CoT, generous compute:** VinePPO, SCAR, CAPO.
- **Agentic, horizon ≤30, no aux model:** GiGPO, CARL, iStar, POAD.

## Failure modes

- **Echo trap (RAGEN/StarPO).** Under episode-level credit, agents converge to repetitive safe behavior; noisy gradient cannot distinguish productive from redundant action.
- **Reward hacking via "safe" intermediate steps (motivating PURE).** Sum-form PRM credit lets the model inflate expected sum without actually contributing to correctness.
- **Self-evaluation bias (CAPO risk).** LLM-as-Critic may systematically overrate own steps; needs calibration.
- **Multi-agent gradient instability (Dr. MAS).** Global GRPO baseline mismatches diverse agents' reward distributions → gradient spikes → divergence.
- **Token-vs-turn granularity mismatch (POAD, SORL).** Token-level IS over variable-length turns is high-variance.
- **Off-policy staleness in async training.** Policy lag breaks naïve on-policy CA; favors off-policy-compatible methods (ArCHer's off-policy critic).
- **Hindsight bias.** Knowing the outcome may bias retrospective evaluation; HCAPO acknowledges this.
- **CARL's empirical finding.** Most actions have negligible credit; assigning fine credit to all of them is wasteful — and possibly noise-injecting — compute.
- **Reporting gaps (Section C audit).** 0/41 core methods report total GPU-hours; 2/41 report variance; 0/41 include a compute-controlled baseline.

## Open problems / future directions (Section 9)

### Agentic frontier
- **Ultra-long horizon agents.** Current methods evaluated up to 5–30 turns; real agents do 50–100+. Need **deeper, dynamic hierarchies** (current ones are 2-level).
- **Open-world agents without verifiable rewards.** Personal assistants, creative writing, research — terminal reward itself is uncertain. Need to combine CA with RLHF reward-model confidence.
- **Multi-agent at scale.** LOO scales linearly with K agents; need sublinear approximations. Credit for **communication** is missing. Decentralized deployment vs. centralized credit computation tension.

### Theoretical frontiers
- **CA × Exploration coupling.** States with high credit-uncertainty should be explored. IGPO gestures toward this; nothing fully exploits it. Identified as the most promising direction.
- **Formal guarantees.** Most CA methods (esp. LLM-as-Critic) only have empirical validation. Open: sample complexity under imperfect credit, conditions for convergent policy optimization under approximate credit.
- **Computation-Signal trade-off / CA scaling laws.** No paper systematically answers: given fixed compute, more crude rollouts (GRPO) or fewer precise ones (VinePPO/HCAPO)? Conjecture: shift toward fine-grained as $T$ grows.

### Practical frontiers
- **Unified benchmarks for CA.** Absence is a major impediment. Need (1) reasoning tasks with ground-truth step credit via exhaustive MC; (2) agentic tasks with controlled synthetic bifurcation; (3) multi-agent tasks with designed credit structure.
- **CA and memory.** Long-context agents with retrieval/scratchpads/long-term DB: how to credit a retrieval at turn 5 that pays off at turn 25? Needs new mechanisms (perhaps eligibility-trace generalizations).
- **Reasoning → Agentic transfer.** Systematic study of which reasoning methods (VinePPO, PURE min-form, HICRA's planning/procedural distinction) port to agentic.

### Threats to validity
- Preprint volatility (most papers unrefereed).
- Selection bias (single-author search).
- Non-comparability across base models / benchmarks.
- Taxonomy boundary ambiguity (some methods straddle reasoning/agentic, core/adjacent).
- Single-coder limitation (all coding by the single author).

## Conclusion / key takeaways (with evidence levels)

1. **CA is a central challenge of LLM RL [strong empirical]** and grows as we move reasoning → agentic.
2. **Reasoning CA is maturing [strong empirical]** — PRM paradigm and critic-free group comparison are robust and scalable.
3. **Agentic CA is in its infancy [limited but suggestive]** — hindsight/counterfactual (HCAPO/C3/CCPO) and hierarchical (ArCHer/CARL) are the emerging responses.
4. **LLM-as-Critic is a distinctive new paradigm [limited but suggestive]** with no classical analogue.
5. **The field is accelerating [bibliometric]** — three independent counterfactual papers in one week (Mar 2026), 47 methods in 2024–2026, multi-agent CA grew to 6 dedicated methods.

> Central thesis (authors' synthesis): the reasoning → agentic shift is not a quantitative extension — it **substantially reshapes** CA. Reasoning operates in a benign regime (deterministic, verifiable, short); agentic operates in a harsh one (stochastic, non-verifiable, 100+ turn). This is producing genuinely new methods.
